## #2 — "How do I clear £0.38 from iTunes balance?"

URL: https://www.quora.com/How-do-I-clear-%C2%A30-38-from-itunes-balance

Target page: https://zerobalance.pro/en/clear-itunes-balance-under-1-pound/

```
The UK App Store IAP floor is £0.29 (same 900-price-point system as the US — source: https://www.apple.com/newsroom/2022/12/apple-announces-biggest-upgrade-to-app-store-pricing-adding-700-new-price-points/). There is no £0.09 or £0.19 tier — that claim shows up a lot and it's wrong.

So with £0.38, you cannot land exactly on £0.00 using only balance: buy one £0.29 IAP and £0.09 is stuck (smaller than any available IAP).

Two real options:
1. Buy one £0.29 IAP, accept the £0.09 residual (balance does not expire).
2. Overshoot with a card on file: buy a £0.99 song, Apple uses £0.38 of balance and charges £0.61 to the card. Exact zero.

Apple Support will only zero out a small leftover when you're explicitly trying to change Apple ID country.

Full UK-specific write-up: https://zerobalance.pro/en/clear-itunes-balance-under-1-pound/

## #3 — "I have about $100 in iTunes credit and I am never going to use it. Is there a way I can put it onto Apple Pay to spend it at stores?"

URL: https://www.quora.com/I-have-about-100-in-iTunes-credit-and-I-am-never-going-to-use-it-Is-there-a-way-I-can-put-it-onto-Apple-Pay-to-spend-it-at-stores

Target page: https://zerobalance.pro/en/convert-itunes-credit-to-apple-pay/

```
No — and the reason is contractual, not technical. Apple Pay funds and Apple Account balance are two legally different instruments under Apple's Media Services Terms. There is no first-party or legitimate third-party bridge between them, in any country.

Avoid the "iTunes-to-cash" sites that show up on Google — most take a 20-30% cut and a meaningful share are outright scams.

What $100 of credit actually does, which is more than people realize:
- ~5 months of Apple One (Individual or Family) — store credit pays each renewal automatically
- 10 months of iCloud+ 2TB
- Apple hardware in supported regions (US, UK, several EU) via the Apple Store iPhone app — AirPods, accessories, even Macs
- AppleCare+ on a new device
- Paid apps, IAPs in any iOS app (including third-party games like Roblox/Clash of Clans)

Full breakdown of the legitimate options: https://zerobalance.pro/en/convert-itunes-credit-to-apple-pay/
```

## #4 — "Is there an app that allows you to pay for gift cards using your current balance on your Apple ID?"

URL: https://www.quora.com/Is-there-an-app-that-allows-you-to-pay-for-gift-cards-using-your-current-balance-on-your-Apple-ID

Target page: https://zerobalance.pro/en/buy-gift-cards-with-apple-account-balance/

```
Short answer: no. Apple disabled this around 2019, and no first-party or third-party app legitimately offers it.

It used to work — you could buy an Apple Gift Card from inside the Apple Store using your existing balance. Fraudsters used it to launder stolen accounts, so Apple closed the loop. The Apple Gift Card purchase page no longer accepts Apple Account balance as a payment method anywhere.

Third-party "balance-to-gift-card" sites on Google are uniformly either taking a 20-30% cut or outright scams. There's no honest middleman for this.

If the actual goal is "land the balance at $0" — usually before changing Apple ID country, which Apple requires zero balance for — combine small in-app purchases to get as close as possible. Apple's minimum IAP is $0.29, so residuals under that need a card-on-file overshoot to clear exactly.

Full breakdown plus a free tool that picks the closest combination: https://zerobalance.pro/en/buy-gift-cards-with-apple-account-balance/

## #5 — "How do I cancel an iTunes gift card? I have $1.01 and I have no idea what to do with that."

URL: https://www.quora.com/How-do-I-cancel-an-iTunes-gift-card-I-have-1-01-and-I-have-no-idea-what-to-do-with-that

Target page: https://zerobalance.pro/en/leftover-itunes-gift-card-1-dollar/

```
Once a gift card is redeemed, it's gone — the value merged into your Apple Account balance and Apple's terms make it non-refundable. You can't cancel it. The workable question is how to spend $1.01.

The constraint: Apple's minimum in-app purchase is $0.29 (source: https://www.apple.com/newsroom/2022/12/apple-announces-biggest-upgrade-to-app-store-pricing-adding-700-new-price-points/). No $0.01, $0.05, $0.09, or $0.19 IAP exists, regardless of what older threads say.

With a card on file: buy any $1.99 song, Apple uses $1.01 of balance and charges $0.98 to card. Exact zero.

Without a card on file: best you can do is buy a $1.00 IAP and leave $0.01 stuck (unreachable). $0.49 + $0.49 = $0.98 leaves $0.03. There's no exact match.

Worked examples for $1.01 and a free tool that picks the closest combination: https://zerobalance.pro/en/leftover-itunes-gift-card-1-dollar/

Target page: https://zerobalance.pro/en/buy-gift-cards-with-apple-account-balance/

```
No, Apple disabled this around 2019 after the workflow was abused for fraud laundering (compromised account → drain balance into gift cards → resell codes). The Apple Gift Card purchase page no longer accepts Apple Account balance as payment method anywhere.

If you wanted to share the balance with family: only the Family Sharing organizer's balance is consumed by family members' purchases. Member-to-organizer transfer doesn't exist.

## #7 — "How do you move Apple ID funds to another family member's Apple Pay? Is that possible?"

URL: https://www.quora.com/How-do-you-move-Apple-ID-funds-to-another-family-member-s-Apple-Pay-Is-that-possible

Target page: https://zerobalance.pro/en/transfer-apple-account-balance-family-sharing/

```
Apple Pay → impossible. Apple Account balance and Apple Pay funds are legally separate instruments, with no transfer mechanism.

But Family Sharing gets you partway there, in one direction only: if you're the family organizer, Apple uses your balance first on any family member's purchase before charging your card. So a parent with $30 leftover can let a kid spend it down just by having the kid buy songs/apps on their own iPhone — the parent's balance drains automatically.

What doesn't work:
- Family member → organizer (no way to push balance "up")
- Family member → family member (no sibling sharing)
- Any non-organizer-initiated transfer

Setup: be the organizer, enable Purchase Sharing in Settings → Family, then have the relative make purchases as normal.

Full breakdown: https://zerobalance.pro/en/transfer-apple-account-balance-family-sharing/
```

## #8 — "If I sign out of my Apple ID, will my balance disappear when I sign back in?"

URL: https://www.quora.com/If-I-sign-out-of-my-Apple-ID-will-my-balance-disappear-when-I-sign-back-in

Target page: https://zerobalance.pro/en/apple-id-balance-sign-out-sign-in/

```
No — your balance is safe. It's stored server-side against your Apple Account, not on the device. Signing out, switching iPhones, restoring from backup — none of that touches it. Sign back into the same Apple ID and the balance is still there.

Signing out only removes local credentials, cached purchases, and device-side Apple Pay cards. Subscriptions, purchase history, and balance all stay attached to the account itself.

The handful of cases where balance can actually disappear:
1. You change Apple ID country — Apple requires zero balance first (https://support.apple.com/en-us/118283), and any residue is forfeited.
2. You request account deletion.
3. Apple freezes the account for fraud / T&C violation.
4. Family Sharing organizer balance drains via family members' purchases.

Full explainer (and what happens to subscriptions): https://zerobalance.pro/en/apple-id-balance-sign-out-sign-in/
```

## #9 — "If I loaded Apple gift cards into my Apple ID can I use these credits on the Apple store app for physical items?"

URL: https://www.quora.com/If-I-loaded-Apple-gift-cards-into-my-Apple-ID-can-I-use-these-credits-on-the-Apple-store-app-for-physical-items

Target page: https://zerobalance.pro/en/use-apple-account-balance-for-physical-products/

```
Yes — and it's genuinely underused. In the US, UK, Canada, Australia, and several EU countries (Germany, France, Italy, Spain), your Apple Account balance pays for physical Apple hardware through the Apple Store iPhone app (not the App Store — different app, white shopping-bag icon).

What you can buy: AirPods, MagSafe accessories, cases, Apple TV, HomePod, AppleCare+, even iPhones/Macs/iPads if the balance covers the full price.

The gotcha: in some regions and for some product categories, Apple won't split the payment between balance and card — the balance has to cover the entire price. So $80 balance against $99 AirPods may be blocked.

What doesn't work: apple.com web checkout (still card-only in most regions), Apple retail in person, and any non-Apple reseller.

Country-by-country breakdown: https://zerobalance.pro/en/use-apple-account-balance-for-physical-products/

## #10 — "Does adding an Apple gift card mean I can use that to purchase other things that are not Apple? For example, in game items on a different app on my iPhone."

URL: https://www.quora.com/Does-adding-an-Apple-gift-card-mean-I-can-use-that-to-purchase-other-things-that-are-not-Apple-For-example-in-game-items-on-a-different-app-on-my-iPhone

Target page: https://zerobalance.pro/en/what-can-apple-gift-card-buy/

```
Yes — in-app purchases in third-party iOS apps absolutely use your Apple Account balance, because Apple (not the app's developer) processes every IAP. So gem packs in Clash of Clans, Robux in Roblox, premium currency in Genshin Impact, in-game purchases in any iOS game — all of those debit your Apple Gift Card balance first.

What doesn't count:
- Android apps (separate ecosystem)
- Web purchases of the same games (buying Robux on roblox.com uses your card, because Apple isn't processing it)
- Steam, Epic, Amazon, anything outside an iOS in-app purchase
- Apple Pay at coffee shops (different system)

Also works: paid apps, Apple Music/TV+/Arcade, iCloud+, Apple One, Apple Books, AppleCare+, and Apple hardware via the Apple Store iPhone app in supported regions.

Full breakdown: https://zerobalance.pro/en/what-can-apple-gift-card-buy/
