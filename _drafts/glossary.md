# Apple Account Balance Glossary: Every Term Explained

_Updated May 2026._

Every term you'll run into while trying to spend, transfer, or clear money on an Apple Account. Definitions are short on purpose - tap any term to jump to a longer guide if there is one.

## Apple Account (formerly Apple ID)

The credential and customer record that owns your purchases, subscriptions, and balance across iCloud, the App Store, iTunes, Apple Music, and Apple's other services. In 2024 Apple renamed "Apple ID" to "Apple Account", but the two terms are used interchangeably.

## Apple Account balance

The pool of store credit attached to your Apple Account. You can spend it on apps, in-app purchases, subscriptions, Apple Music tracks, movies, books, and iCloud Storage upgrades. You cannot withdraw it, transfer it to another account, or convert it to cash. See: [convert Apple ID balance to cash](/en/convert-apple-id-balance-to-cash/).

## Store credit

A generic term for the same thing as Apple Account balance. Used interchangeably in Apple's UI and support pages.

## Gift card

A physical or digital code that, when redeemed, adds funds to your Apple Account balance. Apple gift cards are denominated in the currency of the redeeming storefront. A US gift card redeemed on a US Apple Account adds USD; the same card cannot be used on a UK account.

## Gift card residue

The small leftover that remains after you've spent most of a gift card. Example: redeem $25, buy a $24.53 subscription, leftover is $0.47. This residue is the most common reason people hit the zero-balance problem. See: [leftover Apple gift card](/en/leftover-apple-gift-card/).

## IAP (In-App Purchase)

A purchase made inside an app rather than by buying the app itself. Apple processes all IAPs through the App Store; they are charged against your balance first and your payment method second.

## Consumable IAP

A type of IAP that's used up after purchase and can be bought again. Examples: in-game gems, app credits, single-use boosts. Consumables are what Zero Balance uses to clear small leftovers, because they're the only IAP category with prices below $0.99.

## Non-consumable IAP

A type of IAP you buy once and keep forever. Examples: removing ads in a free app, unlocking a paid feature. Non-consumables can't be purchased twice on the same Apple Account.

## Subscription

A recurring IAP that renews automatically at a fixed interval (weekly, monthly, yearly). Subscriptions deduct from your Apple Account balance first, then charge your payment method for the difference. Half-canceled subscriptions are a common source of leftover balance.

## Storefront

The country-specific version of the App Store you currently see. Each storefront has its own catalog of apps, its own pricing, and its own currency. Your Apple Account belongs to exactly one storefront at a time. See: [changing Apple ID country](/en/change-apple-id-country-with-balance/).

## Region

In Apple's UI, region is interchangeable with storefront. "Change region" and "change country" both mean moving your Apple Account from one storefront to another.

## Pre-flight checklist (for region change)

The list of conditions Apple requires before allowing you to change storefront. The hard one: balance must be exactly $0.00. Others: no active subscriptions, no pending refunds, no pre-orders, no active store credit transfers in flight.

## Family Sharing

A feature that lets up to six Apple Accounts share purchases, subscriptions, iCloud Storage, and certain Apple services. Family Sharing does **not** share Apple Account balance - this is the most-Googled myth in the entire balance ecosystem. See: [the Family Sharing balance myth](/en/family-sharing-apple-balance-myth/).

## Pro-rated charge

A partial charge applied when you start or cancel a subscription mid-period. Pro-rated charges produce balance amounts in fractions of cents that the UI rounds to two decimals. The rounded difference can show up as a stuck remainder of $0.01-$0.03.

## Currency rounding

Apple's internal accounting tracks balance to more decimal places than the UI displays. When you change region or convert between currencies (rare), rounding can leave a fraction of a cent visible. Apple Support is generally willing to absorb rounding residue on request.

## App Store Connect

Apple's portal for developers to manage their apps, pricing, and IAPs. Not user-facing, but mentioned in some support flows ("contact the developer through App Store Connect"). For balance issues, App Store Connect is not where you go - you go to [getsupport.apple.com](https://getsupport.apple.com).

## Apple Refund

Apple's refund flow for individual purchases at [reportaproblem.apple.com](https://reportaproblem.apple.com). When a refund is granted, the money returns to your Apple Account balance as store credit, not to your card - which is itself a source of new leftover balance.

## Storefront-locked credit

Informal term for balance that can only be spent in the storefront where it was added. All Apple Account balance is storefront-locked by default. This is why you can't "carry" your USD balance with you when moving to a UK App Store.

## "Empty balance" / "Zero out balance"

The action of bringing your Apple Account balance to exactly $0.00. The terms appear in Apple Support's internal taxonomy and in the [Apple Discussions](https://discussions.apple.com) topic tagging. See: [reset Apple ID balance](/en/reset-apple-id-balance/).

## Zero Balance (the app)

A free iOS utility that automates spending a leftover Apple Account balance down to exactly $0.00 by picking a combination of small consumable IAPs. _Disclosure: I built it._ [App Store listing.](https://apps.apple.com/app/apple-store/id6761912988?pt=128302223&ct=zerobalance-pro-site&mt=8)
